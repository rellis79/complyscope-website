Why ComplyScope exists

ComplyScope was built from 15 years of hands-on HSEQ practice across Irish construction, infrastructure, and energy projects. The knowledge embedded in the system comes from reviewing thousands of RAMS, safety statements, plant records, and contractor submissions — studying where gaps appear, why delays start, and where liability accumulates.

Construction document review is manual, rushed, and inconsistent. Under programme pressure, people scan paperwork when they should be reading it thoroughly. ComplyScope handles the first pass — not to replace professional judgement, but to give the duty holder structured, consistent output that is actually worth reviewing.

The system is calibrated against Irish construction legislation — not adapted from a generic tool. It was designed around the duty-holder framework Irish projects actually operate under. Documents enter through SharePoint, OneDrive, or direct upload. Gap reports come back. Every decision stays with the duty holder.

AI makes the process fast and consistent. Domain expertise makes it relevant. Human review keeps accountability where it belongs.

Why generic AI isn’t enough

Any AI tool can read a document. But reading it is not the same as knowing what it needs to contain. ComplyScope is calibrated specifically against current Irish construction legislation — built by practitioners with deep site-level HSEQ expertise.

Legislative framework

ComplyScope checks every document against current Irish construction legislation. The regulatory baseline cannot be switched off or overridden.

Safety, Health and Welfare at Work Act 2005Primary health and safety legislation. Safety statements, employer duties, duty holder obligations.
Construction Regulations 2013 (S.I. 291/2013)RAMS requirements, PSCS duties, Fifth Schedule competency, site-specific obligations.
General Application Regulations (S.I. 299/2007)Chemical agents, hazardous substances, PPE, manual handling, work at height.

Safe Pass (SOLAS)Validity, expiry, renewal requirements for all operatives on Irish construction sites.

Questions buyers ask first

Is this an AI system?
Yes. ComplyScope uses AI to read and parse compliance documents. Every finding is checked against a manually maintained library of current Irish construction legislation. The AI handles the reading — the legislation library handles the standard.
Does ComplyScope approve or sign off documentation?
No. ComplyScope identifies gaps and flags them for review. Every approval and sign-off decision remains with your appointed duty holder. The system never passes or fails a document.
How is this different from an audit?
An audit is a formal inspection by a competent person with legal standing. ComplyScope is a document review tool that identifies gaps before a document is approved. It supports your audit trail — it does not replace it.
I already have a Safety Advisor. Why do I need this?
ComplyScope handles the first-pass document review — the most time-consuming part of a Safety Advisor’s desk work. It frees them to be on site rather than at a desk reading paperwork. It does not replace professional judgement.
How does it handle AI errors or hallucinations?
Every finding is cited against a specific regulation. If a finding cannot be cited, it is not included in the report. Your duty holder reviews every output before any action is taken — the system is designed so a missed gap is caught at review, not acted on unchecked.
How does the system connect to my document workflow?
Documents can be uploaded directly through the dashboard, or the system can connect to a designated SharePoint or OneDrive folder. New documents dropped into the folder are processed automatically. No change to your existing workflow is required.
